# Budget & Upgrade Priorities (Guide)

Last reviewed: 2026-04-18  
Status: internal guidance - update with your school/vendor requirements when available.

## If you have a budget around $800–$1200

If you’re choosing where to spend extra money, the upgrades that usually feel the most noticeable are:

1) RAM
- Prefer 16 GB RAM over 8 GB if you can.
- 8 GB can be “minimum” for light work, but it can feel tight once you have many browser tabs + Office + Zoom.

2) Storage
- Prefer a 512 GB SSD over 256 GB if you can.
- 256 GB is workable, but fills up quickly with updates, school files, and apps.

3) CPU (processor)
- After RAM + storage, a better CPU helps overall responsiveness.
- If you don’t know what you need, “midrange CPU” is usually fine for typical college workloads.

4) GPU (graphics)
- Don’t pay for a dedicated GPU unless you know you need 3D/design/video editing/gaming.

## Minimum vs recommended

- “Minimum specs” means “should run.”
- “Recommended” means “runs comfortably and won’t feel slow as you add more apps and tabs.”

## What to ask the student (so we don’t guess)

- “Do you have any exam/proctoring software requirements (Examplify/Respondus/etc.)?”
- “Do you need Windows-only apps for your major?”
- “Is battery life important (long lecture days)?”
